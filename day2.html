Morning


Take a stroll around Le Botanique before heading to the Comics Art Museum. After shopping on the Rue Neuve, enjoy a local meal in one of the many cafes in the area .

Le Botanique


In 1797, the city of Brussels created a botanical garden in the Palais de Lorraine for botanical students. The main structure is that of a grand glass and iron rotunda at the centre of the gardens designed by Gineste, a French architect and a 19 th century statuary depicting the Four Seasons. The beautiful glasshouse is now home to the French Community Cultural Centre and offers concerts and art exhibitions.

Comics Arts Museum


The Comics Arts Museum is a representation for the passion of the Belgian passion for comic strips. The collection is housed in a Horta-designed Art Nouveau building , the most famous being TInin and the Smurfs. Other displays are that of initial ideas to final production as well as valuable archives of photographs and artifacts.

Rue de Neuve


Shoppers flock to the busy Rue Neuve for its exceptional and reasonably priced goods. It is the heart of commercial shopping in Brussels which has shops, cafes and the media storeFnac all under one roof. To the east is the Place des Martyrs which commemorates the 450 citizens lost during the 1830 uprise

Afternoon

The Quartier Royal is home to a wealth of palaces and parks, the finest being Palais Royal and the Cathedrale Sts Michel et Gudule. Don't linger too long here because the treasures of the Musees Royaux des Beaux-Arts awaits you!

Quartier Royal

The home of Bruseels' nobility and rulers, it was chosen because of the purer air, it was occupied by the 15-th century Coudenburg Palace, home to the Dukes of Brabant and Renaissance rulers. In 1731, the palace was burnt down but slowly recovered during the 18th and 19th centuries. Today, the royal quarter presents elegance with some of Europe's finest 18th century buildings.

Musées Royaux des Beaux-Arts

The Musées Royaux des Beaux-Arts covers 3 main museums the most spectacular and antique being Musee Old Masters. Dating back to the Napoleanic era and few valuable works left behind by the French Republican army. It is best known for their 15th- to 18th-century Flemish art in the world.
